Briards

A HEART WRAPPED IN FUR

The briard,or berger de brie, is a large breed of dog traditionally used as a herder and guardian of sheep.

Breed History

The briard, which is believed to have originated in France, has been bred for centuries to herd and to protect sheep. The breed was
used by the French Army as sentries, messangers and to search for wounded soldiers because of its fine scene of hearing, Braids
were used in the First World War almost to the point of extinction, Currently the population of briards is slowly recovering.
Charlemangne, Napolean, Thomas Jefferson and Lafayette all owned briards.
